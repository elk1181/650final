Final Thoughts

This section will go over my final findings on the groups discussed as well as goals for further research.

To Summarize

While Glee was praised for its depiction of underepresented groups, when actually examining specific margelinzed groups, the show did not do as well as it seemed

Both groups described in research showed that underrepresentated groups being shown on television is not enough for proper representation. The groups researched both had lack of backstories outside of their underrepresentation. The storylines they had often had them seen as second to white-able club members and it is never mentioned as being wrong, with the ramifications never being aknowledged.

Continuous satire comments

Another area that was mentioned throughout the research found was the continous mocking comments that were made towards minority characters. Tina and Mike were continously referred to as "Asian" and "Other-Asian" and Artie was often called a person and a "half", "cripple", or "wheels". All though it was portrayed as a satire, is is never seen or aknowledged as being bad with the name-calling casually occuring consistently in different episodes.

To Conclude: Season 1 Episode 7, "Throwdown"

To truly understand the findings in a summarized manner, clips will be used to conclude this research from the episode, Throwdown.

Season 1's episode "Throwdown" focuses on Sue using the underrepresented students in order to turn the Glee club against Will.

(Glee Scenes 2017)

In the scene, the character Sue takes the underepresented students from the Glee club to start her own. She refers to them by a series of offensive names in order to bring them to her side, all while claiming she is trying to give them more of a voice as Will does not provide opportunites for those students. While this is seen as untrue and a way to destroy Will's reputation, a later scene in the episode makes this confusing.
